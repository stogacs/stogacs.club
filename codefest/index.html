About CodeFest III

The CodeFest III is a month-long competition between Conestoga students! You will be tasked to code any project you desire—be it game, app, or website. You can choose to work in a team of up to four, or go solo.

The Rules

The rules of CodeFest are the following:
  1. There will be a max of four members per team. If you form a large team and win a challenge, every member will be award Shekels, but other winnings could be less for each person.
  2. Teams can of course gain advice and support from teachers. In other words, we do allow asking for advice, clarification, and language/syntax assistance from the computer science teachers Mr. Dewees and Mr. Sharick, non-participants from the club, or any other coding expert you know of. We also strongly encourage asking for help if you are stuck on coming up with ideas. However, all work must be your team’s. Experts can only give guidance and are prohibited from coding anything for you. Teams may not work with each other. Copying code of any kind is strictly prohibited.
  3. All work on a project should be done during CodeFest.
  4. Teams can use an idea they had before CodeFest.
  5. Teams can work on ideas that have already been done. Projects do not strictly have to be “innovative”. If somebody wants to work on a common idea they should be allowed to do so and should be judged on the quality of their project. These days it’s hard to find something that’s fully original and teams might not know an idea has been done before anyway.
  6. Teams can use libraries, frameworks, or open-source code in their projects. Working on a project before the event and open-sourcing it for the sole purpose of using the code during the event is against the spirit of the rules and is not allowed. Teams must indicate which external libraries are used and which portions of the submitted project are original and which are external.
  7. Teams must stop coding once the time is up. The submission is the final version of the project.
  8. Teams can be disqualified from the competition at the organizers' discretion. Reasons might include but are not limited to plagiarizing, unsporting behaviour, or breaking any other competition rules.

How will the projects be judged?

CREATIVITY (35)

Is your project creative? If not wholly original, does it have elements of innovation? Does it have aspects not seen in any other current day product? 

COMPLEXITY (30)

How complex is the functionality of your project? Is it just wrapping an existing library, or does it implement difficult logic or computations itself?

POLISH (15)

How well does your project work? Does it even run? Do all functions work as intended, or are there obvious bugs? Is it unwieldy and clunky, or is it intuitive and smooth to use?

CODE QUALITY (20)

How readable is your code? Is there lots of obvious code smell? Does it reflect extensive knowledge of the tools and languages used? Are we able to compile your project on our own computers relatively easily?

Why should I participate?

CodeFest is our biannual Stoga-hosted computer science competition. It aims to show that coding can be a fun and enjoyable activity– providing a different setting for programming than what you may be used to. And, since time is money, we want to offer real prizes for impressive efforts and incentivize putting forth your best. All the prizes will be supplied by the Conestoga Computer Science Club. We encourage everyone to participate, even those not confident in their coding skills. Working with friends on a fun project is a valuable learning experience!
